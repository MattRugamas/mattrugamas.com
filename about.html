--- title: About ---

I'm Matt Rugamas,

a support engineer based in Los Ángeles, working at the intersection of customers, code and product.

Over the past 8 years, I have worked through systems end to end. APIs, logs, authentication flows, databases, and when needed, application code. The goal is simple. Find the root cause and make it understandable.

Most recently at Atlassian, I worked on JSM Chat, owning escalations and partnering closely with engineers during incidents. A lot of my work focused on turning unclear issues into actionable fixes and helping the team get better at debugging as the product evolved.

What I enjoy most is the translation layer. Taking something messy and turning it into something clear. For engineers, that means direction. For customers, it means clarity and trust.

This site is a mix of a personal space and a technical notebook. It started as a place to document how I built it, and it is where I write about debugging, systems, and the kinds of problems I tend to get pulled into. I kept it simple on purpose so the focus stays on the thinking.

I am currently open to new opportunities where support goes beyond tickets and into understanding, improving, and shaping the system itself.

Outside of work, I spend time with music, web projects, and long bike rides.
