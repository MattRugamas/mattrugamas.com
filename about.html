--- title: About ---

Hey, I'm Matthew Rugamas.

Professionally, I'm a Technical Support Engineer at Cursor, based in Los Angeles. I've spent my career helping people untangle complex problems, building and advocating for accessible experiences and making sure products work not just in theory, but for real people with real constraints.

Personally, I'm a lifelong student, a musician and a road cyclist who thinks some of the best ideas show up somewhere between mile 10 and mile 40. I love learning how things work, whether it's systems, instruments or people, then finding better ways to connect them. When I'm not debugging an issue or diving into AI tech, you'll probably find me practicing, reading or planning the next ride I'm not quite in shape for yet.

Beyond titles, I'm a son, a brother, a grandchild and a friend. The way I show up at work is shaped by these relationships. I care about patience, kindness, clear communication and making space for people to be fully themselves. If there's a theme running through my life, it's that I like helping people feel more capable, whether that's through a solved ticket, an accessible interface, a shared song or a really good conversation.

This site is a mix of a personal space and a technical notebook. It started as a place to document how I built it, and it is now where I write about thoughts, opinions, debugging, systems and the kinds of problems I tend to get pulled into.
