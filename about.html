--- title: About ---

Hey, I'm Matthew Rugamas,

a support engineer based in Los Ángeles who turns messy customer escalations into clear technical direction. For nearly a decade I've worked across systems end to end — APIs, logs, authentication flows, databases, and when needed, application code — to find root causes and make them understandable.

Day to day I'm in Splunk, Jira, Postman, LaunchDarkly, MongoDB and the codebase itself, depending on where the answer is hiding.

Most recently at Atlassian on JSM Chat, I owned escalations and partnered closely with engineers during incidents. The debugging SOPs, playbooks and training I designed there reduced escalations by 15% year over year by enabling engineers to resolve issues without escalation.

What I enjoy most is the translation layer. Taking something messy and turning it into something clear. For engineers, that means direction. For customers, it means clarity and trust.

This site is a mix of a personal space and a technical notebook. It started as a place to document how I built it, and it is where I write about debugging, systems, and the kinds of problems I tend to get pulled into. I kept it simple on purpose so the focus stays on the thinking.

I'm currently open to new opportunities where support goes beyond tickets and into understanding, improving and shaping the system itself.

Outside of work, I spend time with music, web projects, and long bike rides.
